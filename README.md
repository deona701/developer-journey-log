# developer-journey-log

this is a tracked log of my learning journey before i go to university.
for context, i started this because of a math competition that i had. i realized i wanted to be better, and i have been working towards goals similar to the one im working towards now, but without a full plan in mind. 
when i went to the math competition, i failed and only got 5 points. i wasnt serious, and i didnt study enough. the 5 points didn't really hit me that hard. i was mostly disappointed in myself for not trying. so i decided id start trying.
i had a physics competition coming up and i managed to pass to regionals. i studied hard for 6 weeks (all logged), but i didn't study as i was supposed to. instead of studying olympiad questions, i studied theory. i only realized this 2 weeks before the exam, and i solved around 60 questions in the span of one week, without counting the questions i re-solved (which would likely increase it by around 10-20). either way, it unfortunately was not enough, as i had 37 points, and the points needed to pass were 40. i did not mind. my only goal was to remain in that class the whole test, which i did. after that, i didn't care if i won or lost. i proved to myself that i was capable of discipline, which was the most important thing to me. and it didn't stop there, and it was never supposed to stop there.


The Rules of My System:                           

• time boxed execution: every subject is strictly limited to 30, 40, or 60-minute blocks to optimise cognitive load and task-switching. the only way this rule is broken is if i have the time in the day to break it, and it often only applies to creative subjects because i don't want to burn out. my schedule is optimised to the point where if i do any more when it comes to programming, math or physics, it's endgame. burn out. which is why i don't do more.
• the 2 skip rule: i have the ability to skip two things weekly with absolutely no guilt, no questions, no matter the reason for the skip.
• the most important thing: to have fun. i wouldn't be doing all of this if it wasnt fun. you'll see that throughout this journey ive tried a number of things and left behind a good bunch, too. if i don't enjoy something, i never force myself unless i know its for my own good and it's better to know it than to not.


note: the earlier logs (february to early/mid may) do not contain any commentary. this is a personal choice as my commentary is private and feels more like a diary to me, and i considered filtering it but i think it would be better to not waste time. i was planning on posting the logs, but i only now decided i would do so, which is why they are unfiltered. however, commentary will be shown in other logs as to give a better view of why i do things the way i do, and why i make the changes that i make.
